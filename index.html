    The Unrelenting Toll: Leading Pollution Caused by Vehicles

    In recent decades, the global surge in urbanization and industrialization has given rise to a significant increase in vehicular traffic, leading to a surge in pollution levels. Vehicles, while indispensable for modern life, have emerged as one of the leading contributors to environmental degradation.

    Particulate Matter (PM)

    Released by combustion engines, these tiny particles penetrate deep into the respiratory system, causing respiratory and cardiovascular problems.

    Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)

    Nitrogen Oxides (NOx): Vehicle emissions release NOx, contributing to ground-level ozone and smog, leading to respiratory problems and exacerbating existing conditions.

    Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Hydrocarbons

    Incomplete engine combustion releases these pollutants, contributing to air pollution and posing adverse health effects.
